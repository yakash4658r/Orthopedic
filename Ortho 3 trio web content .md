 ABOUT US

About Ortho3Trio

 Ortho3Trio began with a simple observation:

 patients rarely have just "an orthopaedic problem." They have a knee that won't bend, a back that won't straighten, or a shoulder that won't stay put — and each of those deserves a surgeon who has spent years on exactly that structure, not a generalist spreading attention across the entire skeleton.

 Three senior surgeons at Dr. Rela Institute & Medical Centre — each already an established name in their own right — came together to build that focused, collaborative model of care. Dr. Prakash Ayyadurai leads sports injury, arthroscopy and ligament reconstruction. Dr. Parthasarathy Srinivasan leads spine surgery. Dr. Ashok S. Gavaskar leads joint replacement, trauma and complex reconstruction. Together, they cover the orthopaedic spectrum from spine to sole — which is where the name comes from.

 What Makes Us Different  
True sub-specialisation, not three general orthopaedists sharing a waiting room  
 \- Cross-consultation on complex cases — when an injury overlaps specialties, our surgeons confer directly  
 \- One hospital ecosystem — imaging, anaesthesia, physiotherapy and inpatient care all coordinated under Rela Hospital, Chromepet  
 \- Evidence-first medicine — each surgeon is active in research, teaching and professional bodies in their field

Our Shared Philosophy  
 Ortho3Trio was founded on the belief that orthopaedic care should never feel like a conveyor belt. Every patient — whether a weekend athlete, a working professional with back pain, or someone finally ready for a joint replacement — deserves a plan built around their life, explained in plain language, and delivered by the person best qualified to deliver it.

. About — Dr. Prakash Ayyadurai

 Dr. Prakash Ayyadurai  
 Subtitle: Sports Medicine, Arthroscopy & Ligament Reconstruction Specialist

 Dr. Prakash Ayyadurai specialises in arthroscopy and ligament reconstruction of the knee, shoulder, ankle, wrist and hip. He currently serves as Clinical Lead, Arthroscopy & Sports Medicine at Dr. Rela Institute & Medical Centre, and is a Visiting Professor at Sri Ramachandra Centre for Sports Science. He is also the architect and co-convener of the Indian Ligament Registry — a national effort to track and improve outcomes in ligament surgery.

 Dr. Prakash was among the first surgeons in India to introduce synthetic ACL and PCL ligament reconstruction, and Tamil Nadu's first to use a balloon spacer implant for irreparable rotator cuff tears. To date, he has performed over 4,000 ACL reconstructions and 2,000+ shoulder dislocation repairs.

 An Athlete's Understanding of Recovery  
 Before he was a surgeon, Dr. Prakash was a competitive athlete — captaining his state in field hockey, winning university football championships, and completing marathons in Chennai, Berlin and Paris. That background shapes how he treats patients: not just as an injury to fix, but as a comeback to plan. He has worked closely with elite athletes and sporting bodies, bringing that same intensity of care to every patient who walks in, from professional sportspeople to weekend joggers.

 Focus Areas

 \- Shoulder arthroscopy & instability repair  
 \- Cartilage and biologic joint restoration  
 \- Multi-ligament and complex knee reconstruction  
 \- Sports injury management & rehabilitation  
 \- Synthetic ligament surgery (Jewel ACL™, InternalBrace)

 About — Dr. Parthasarathy Srinivasan

 Dr. Parthasarathy Srinivasan  
 Subtitle: Spine Surgeon

 Qualifications: D.Ortho, DNB (Ortho), FNB (Spine Surgery), MNAMS

 Dr. Parthasarathy Srinivasan is a spine surgeon based at Dr. Rela Institute & Medical Centre, Chromepet, with more than 12 years of focused experience and over 1,500 successful spine surgeries to his name. He founded Total Spine with a clear goal — to make expert, accurate spine diagnosis and treatment accessible to patients across Chennai and beyond, while actively working to dispel common myths around back pain and spine surgery.

 In 2009, Dr. Parthasarathy was selected as one of only eight candidates from across Asia-Pacific Orthopaedic Association member countries for the prestigious APOA DePuy Spine Travelling Fellowship — recognition of his standing among the region's spine specialists early in his career.

A Preventive-First, Precision-Second Approach  
 Dr. Parthasarathy's practice is built on a simple principle: surgery is the answer only when conservative care genuinely can't help. Where surgery is needed, he leans on minimally invasive and endoscopic techniques wherever appropriate — aiming for the same surgical outcome with less tissue disruption, shorter hospital stays, and faster return to daily life.

 Focus Areas  
 \- Spine diagnostics & non-surgical spine care  
 \- Minimally invasive spine surgery (MISS)  
 \- Endoscopic spine surgery  
 \- Disc surgery & disc replacement (cervical and lumbar)  
 \- Spine fracture management  
 \- Spine infection treatment  
 \- Spinal deformity correction (kyphosis, scoliosis)  
 \- Computer-navigated ("surgical GPS") spine procedures

 Areas of Expertise

 Ortho3Trio organises care around the way injuries and conditions actually present — not around a single generalist trying to cover everything. Below are our three core specialty pillars. Each is led by the surgeon who has spent their career on exactly that problem.

  Pillar 1 — Sports Injury, Arthroscopy & Ligament Reconstruction  
 \*Led by Dr. Prakash Ayyadurai\*

 If your knee gives way mid-run, your shoulder keeps slipping out of place, or a sports injury just won't fully heal, this is where you start. Using arthroscopic (keyhole) techniques, we treat ACL, PCL and multi-ligament injuries, recurrent shoulder dislocations, cartilage damage, and rotator cuff tears — with an emphasis on getting active patients, from athletes to weekend players, back to full function.

 Key procedures: ACL/PCL reconstruction · Synthetic ligament surgery · Shoulder arthroscopy · Cartilage restoration · Rotator cuff repair (including InSpace balloon implant) · Sports rehabilitation programs

 Pillar 2 — Spine Care & Spine Surgery  
 Led by Dr. Parthasarathy Srinivasan

 Back and neck pain rarely need surgery first — but when they do, precision matters. This pillar covers everything from disc degeneration and slipped discs to spinal fractures, infections, and deformities like scoliosis. Minimally invasive and endoscopic approaches are used wherever clinically appropriate, to minimise tissue disruption and speed up recovery.

 \*\*Key procedures:\*\* Disc surgery & disc replacement · Minimally invasive spine surgery · Endoscopic spine surgery · Spinal fracture & deformity correction · Computer-navigated spine surgery
